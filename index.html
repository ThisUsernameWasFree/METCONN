ABOUT THE METCONN PROJECT.

It is well established that as we get older, so does our brain.
But some brains age differently than others!

No matter how fast your brain ages though, we know that Brain-Aging almost always result in:

  • Slower Information Processing.
  • Poorer Memory.
  • Difficulty paying attention.
  • Changes in Decision-making.
  • Higher risks of Dementia.


But why does this happen and why are some people more vulnerable to these changes than others?

The METCONN Project is a scientific study that aims to answer these questions.

Our team of researchers theorise that many of the mental-changes brought about by aging could be the result of metabolic changes in the brain, causing changes in how the brain uses the energy provided by the sugar and oxygen that we eat and breathe.

To test this theory, the Metabolic Connectivity (or METCONN) team at Monash Biomedical Imaging are using state-of-the-art neuroimaging technology and blood analysis tools to measure how the brains of 20-to-90 year-old's use the sugar and oxygen they take in.

By learning more about how the brain uses energy as it gets older, the METCONN team may discover new ways to keep our brains younger and healthier for longer.
