ABOUT THE METCONN STUDY.

It is well established that as we get older, so does our brain.
But some brains age differently than others!


No matter how fast your brain ages though, we know that Brain-Ageing almost always results in:

  • Slower Information Processing.
  • Poorer Memory.
  • Difficulty paying attention.
  • Changes in Decision-making.
  • Higher risk of Dementia.


But why does this happen and why are some people more vulnerable to these changes than others?

The METCONN Project is a scientific study that aims to answer these questions.

Our team of researchers theorise that many of the mental changes brought about by ageing could be the result of changes in how the brain uses energy provided by sugar and oxygen.

To test this theory, the Metabolic Connectivity (or METCONN) team at Monash Biomedical Imaging are using state-of-the-art neuroimaging technology and blood analysis tools to measure how the brains of 20-to-90 year-old's use the sugar and oxygen that fuels the brain.

By learning more about how the brain uses energy as it gets older, the METCONN team may discover new ways to keep our brains younger and healthier for longer.
